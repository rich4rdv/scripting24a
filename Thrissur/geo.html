It is the third largest urban agglomeration in Kerala after Kochi and Kozhikode, and the 21st largest in India. The city is built around a 65-acre hillock called Thekkinkadu Ground which seats the most famous Vadakkunnathan temple as its centre. It is located 189 miles north-west of the state's capital city.

Geologically is composed of Archaean gneisses and crystalline schists with major parts covered by Archaean rocks. Thrissur is also home to some of the oldest mosques, temples, and churches in the country. Sharing a coastline on one side and a Mountain range on the other, Thrissur becomes the one place every explorer should once visit. With its wide variety of forests, valleys, waterfalls, hills, backwaters and more, Thrissur continues grows rapidly while preserving its natural beauty.
